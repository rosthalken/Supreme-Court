
I join Justice Stevens' dissent except for footnote 5. Although the principles of stare decisis are not inflexible, I believe they bind the Court here. I reached a similar conclusion in Arizona v. Gant, ante, at 354-355, 129 S. Ct. 1710, 173 L. Ed. 2d 485 (2009) (dissenting opinion), and in several other recent cases. See, e.g.,Leegin Creative Leather Prods. Inc. v. PSKS, Inc., 551 U.S. 877, 923–929, 127 S. Ct. 2705, 168 L. Ed. 2d 623 (2007) (same); Parents Involved in Cmty. Sch. v. Seattle     Sch. Dist. No. 1, 551 U.S. 701, 865–866, 127 S. Ct. 2738, 168 L. Ed. 2d 508 (2007) (same); FEC v. Wis. Right to Life, Inc., 551 U.S. 449, 534–536, 127 S. Ct. 2652, 168 L. Ed. 2d 329 (2007) (Souter, J., dissenting); Bowles v. Russell, 551 U.S. 205, 219-220, 127 S. Ct. 2360, 168 L. Ed. 2d 96 (2007) (Souter, J., dissenting); Gonzales v. Carhart, 550 U.S. 124, 190-191, 127 S. Ct. 1610, 167 L. Ed. 2d 480 (2007) (Ginsburg, J., dissenting); District of Columbia v. Heller, 554 U.S. 570, 675–679, 128 S. Ct. 2783, 171 L. Ed. 2d 637 (2008)    (Stevens, J., dissenting).
